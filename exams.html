The one thing to know

She reuses the same exam, repackaged

Across roughly eight sittings the same handful of questions keep coming back, often word for word. The topics never really change, only the wrapping. Three things are true of almost every paper she sets:

🧍

Always about you

Almost every question asks about your company, your decisions, your opinion. Generic textbook answers score little.

✍️

Open essays

Open-ended writing, 3 hours, four broad questions. She rewards applying ideas to your real job, not reciting definitions.

📊

Tables & reflection

She expects a strategy table, and most papers end with a reflective question. Easy marks if prepared.

What the papers point to. The core of what she draws from sits in Chapters 1, 2, 7, 8 and 9 (Dr. Ghada said she likes Chapter 9 from the last lecture), and the 2026 AI-style paper is the most important one to solve. The pattern analysis here agrees: that AI paper is the most likely next exam, with the classic four-question paper as the strong backup. So both are answered in full below.
